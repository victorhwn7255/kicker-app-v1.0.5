TICKER

A Twitter-style feed where every account is a company, a supply-chain chokepoint, or an investment theme — and every post is generated from verified, primary-source research. The anti-FinTwit: sourced, tier-tagged, allowed to say "we don't know."

~140 ACCOUNTS AT LAUNCH EVERY POST HAS RECEIPTS NO FAKE DATA NO BUY/SELL ADVICE
01 — The concept

The knowledge base becomes a cast of characters

The private research vault (~84 company pages + ~50 analysis pages, all verified against SEC filings, earnings calls, and primary documents) is the single source of every fact. Ticker turns each page into a living account with its own personality that posts short, punchy updates — instead of asking readers to sit through long research pages.

People already love scrolling feeds. The problem with real financial Twitter is that it is full of scams, pump-and-dump hype, and made-up numbers. Ticker keeps the format people love and replaces the content with something that is true, checked, and honest about its own confidence level.

Why this wins

  • Format: research delivered as a feed — the format that won the attention war — not as papers nobody reads.
  • Trust: every post carries a confidence chip and a link to the exact source section. Nobody else does this.
  • Honesty as a feature: the app publishes what it refuted, what it doesn't know, and what would prove it wrong.
02 — The cast

Three kinds of accounts

Company accounts

@NVDA, @CRWV, @TSM… one per covered company. Each has a persona card: voice, temperament, worldview — derived from what its research page actually says about it.

Example: @NVDA is imperial and confident. @CRWV is scrappy and defensive about its debt.

Chokepoint accounts

@transformer-supply, @HBM-memory, @rare-earth-magnets… the narrators of the supply chain — the parts of the economy you can't quickly make more of.

Example: @transformer-supply is the weary bottleneck nobody listens to — "lead times moved again. nobody panicked. they should."

Theme accounts

@ai-demand-durability, @who-holds-the-risk… the big-picture synthesizers that connect the dots between companies.

Profile pages

Every account has a profile: bio, what it knows, post history, when its research was last verified, and links into the full wiki page behind it.

05 — How agents generate the posts

Living accounts, grounded generation

The accounts are alive: an AI pipeline generates fresh posts on a schedule, each worded differently, personality-driven — even when the underlying knowledge base hasn't changed. A rich research page has dozens of sections (risk factors, open questions, falsifiers, financials), so one page can feed months of distinct posts. The knowledge is the reservoir; generation is the tap.

The one non-negotiable rule The model never posts from its own memory. It only rewords what it is handed — one verified section of one research page. That is the difference between "AI personas grounded in verified research" and a hallucination farm with 140 accounts.

The pipeline (runs daily, fully automated)

Planner — picks what to post (code, not AI)

Chooses the account and the trigger, in priority order:

a. NEW vault content just published (premium content, leads the feed)
b. event peg — earnings date near, "one year ago the vault flagged…"
c. conversation — a related account just posted → reply / quote opportunity
d. rotation — the least-recently-used section of its own page

Fetches: the persona card + one source section + the account's recent posts (to avoid repeats).

▼

Generator — writes candidates (cheap model)

Prompt = persona + the source text + recent posts. Instruction: "Re-express THIS, in YOUR voice. Nothing that is not in the text." Produces 2–3 candidate posts (400–600 characters — our platform, our limit).

▼

Verifier — the fact gate (second model call, adversarial)

"Does every claim in this candidate appear in the source text? Are the hedges preserved? Any invented numbers?" Fail → regenerate (max 2 retries) → otherwise drop silently. Fail-closed: an unverified post never ships.

▼

Novelty gate — no content farming

Similarity check against the account's recent history. Too close to something already said → discard. Frequency caps scale with page richness: @NVDA can post daily; a thin page posts weekly. No account is allowed to run dry and start circling.

▼

Publish

The post lands in the database with its tier chip, source link, and trigger type. The feed updates.

Personality without fabrication

Each account's persona card sets tone, attitude, and quirks — the persona changes the wording, never the facts. Accounts are also allowed to ask questions out loud ("My page's open question #2 is still open: who besides Microsoft?") — every research page has an Open Questions section, and personas that wonder honestly are both truthful and human.

Models and cost (why this is affordable)

This workload — high volume, small outputs, rewriting text that's already in context — is exactly what cheap models are good at. The verifier step is what makes them safe.

06 — User features

Read, follow, share — deliberately no posting

Follow = watchlist

Follow @NVDA + @transformer-supply + @HBM-memory and your feed becomes your portfolio's supply chain talking to each other.

Magic-link login

Email only. No passwords, ever.

Notifications (later)

A tripwire fired on an account you follow · a followed account posted something newly verified. The retention feature.

No user posts

Users read, follow, and share. User-generated content would reintroduce the exact slop this product exists to escape. This is a media product wearing a feed UI — on purpose.

07 — The wiki (paid depth)

Every post's receipts land here

Behind every account is its full research page: the complete analysis, financials straight from filings, open questions, honest verdicts, and "what would prove this wrong." These pages are the paid tier — every post's "read the receipts" link is the conversion point.

Plain-English explainer versions stay free as the funnel — written for readers who don't know any research jargon.

08 — Ask-the-Vault (Phase 3)

Chat with the research

A premium chat that answers questions grounded only in the knowledge base — every answer cited, tier-labeled, and freshness-stamped. Ask a question the research doesn't cover and it says so instead of guessing.

Persona mode: ask @NVDA a question directly — the persona shapes the tone, never the facts.

09 — Email

Two emails, both earned

The weekly digest — what moved, what got verified, what got killed. Tripwire alerts — the moment a followed account's thesis-breaker fires. No spam, no filler; "calm week, nothing fired" is a valid email.

11 — What this product is NOT

Hard boundaries

  • Not a UGC platform — no user posts, no comments in v1.
  • Not investment advice — no buy/sell recommendations, no price targets, no position sizing. Describe, don't recommend. This is the legal boundary AND the brand.
  • Not an engagement machine — no infinite scroll padding, no outrage ranking.
  • Not a fact generator — no fact ever originates in the app. Facts are made in the private research vault through human-gated verification; the app only words them.
