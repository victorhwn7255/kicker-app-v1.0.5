TICKER Design rationale · 1 page

Eight decisions, and the principles they serve.

Ticker's brief said the design principles override generic best practice. Every choice below is traceable to one. The neo-brutalist system isn't a style — it's the argument that nothing here is decoration; everything is load-bearing.

1
The trust band is structure, not metadata.

Every post ends in a 2px-bordered cream band carrying the tier chip, the receipt link, and the freshness stamp. Crop any single post and its credibility travels with the screenshot — the way posts actually spread on real X.

→ P2 · Trust surfaces are first-class UI
2
One tier stamp, everywhere, readable in grayscale.

A two-cell chip — glyph (✓ / ~ / ✕) plus label — is identical on posts, wiki sections, the kill list, and chat. The word carries the meaning; color only reinforces it, so it survives a screenshot, a small size, and colour-blindness.

→ P2 + P7 · systematic & accessible
3
The feed ends on purpose.

No like / repost / view counts anywhere, no infinite scroll, no pull-to-refresh spinner. The black "You're caught up" card is a designed terminator — the product is proud to stop.

→ P1 · Calm over engagement
4
Receipts are exactly one tap.

"source: … →" opens the exact research section it came from — shown expanded in the permalink's receipt panel and wired live in the daily-loop prototype. Claim to source, one interaction, no "trust me."

→ P3 · Receipts one tap away
5
Nothing on screen implies advice.

No buy/sell, no price targets, no red/green price flashing, no sentiment meters, no P&L. A fired tripwire delivers the fact and stops — the flow ends at understanding, never a recommendation.

→ P4 · Describe, don't recommend
6
Avatars are systematic; the wit lives in words.

Monogram tiles keyed by kind (company = white + ticker, chokepoint = black + code, theme = cream + glyph) scale to 140 accounts and read as identity. We showed pixel-art too, and recommend against it: a legible ticker is information; a mascot is decoration.

→ P5 · Personality lives in the text
7
The paywall is honest.

Section 1 reads free, in plain text with a hard edge — no blur. The gate states exactly what Reader unlocks and the price; locked sections stay legible as titles with their tiers. No countdown, no fake scarcity, no dark pattern.

→ P6 · The paywall is honest
8
The brutalist system is the brand argument.

2px black borders, 0px radius, one hard shadow, cream paper, loud yellow chrome over quiet content. Blunt honesty as a brand becomes a blunt design language — and the border vocabulary makes an honest 2px focus state natural. Every colour pairing meets WCAG AA.

→ P7 · Accessible by default · nothing is decoration
The test that matters most

Does one screenshotted post carry its own credibility? By construction, yes — handle, kind, tier, receipt, and freshness all live inside the card. The rest of the product is that card's promise, kept on every surface.
